Frequently Asked Questions

When an article is supported by reliable, independent sources that meet Wikipedia's verifiability and notability standards, publication becomes a highly achievable outcome. We specialize in identifying and documenting those sources, drafting neutral, well-structured article text, and aligning submissions with Wikipedia's editorial expectations to give your entry the strongest possible chance of acceptance.

We cannot guarantee approval, as Wikipedia maintains independent editorial review processes. What we do guarantee is professional assessment, strategic guidance, and honest evaluation of your eligibility. We help you build the strongest possible foundation.

We offer hands-on drafting and editing support to prepare high-quality, neutral, and well-sourced article drafts. Our team helps structure content, improve citations, and guide edits so your entry meets Wikipedia's standards. We can collaborate on edits and support the submission process, but final posting and acceptance are determined by the Wikipedia community and cannot be guaranteed.

Initial assessment typically takes 1-2 weeks. Comprehensive research and analysis varies based on complexity. Wikipedia approval timelines depend on editorial review, which can range from weeks to months. We provide realistic timelines after the initial assessment.

Start with our assessment wizard. We evaluate notability based on Wikipedia's Verifiability and Notability guidelines. Key factors include independent media coverage, reliable sources, and establishment of significance. Our assessment provides clear, actionable feedback on your eligibility.

If Wikipedia isn't the right outlet or an article faces editorial limits, we create a strategic presence across complementary platforms, including Wikinews, Wikidata, WikiAlpha, EverybodyWiki, and other specialist wikis. We draft and submit news-style coverage to Wikinews when there's timely, reliable reporting; build and curate structured, queryable knowledge in Wikidata to improve discoverability; prepare community-friendly drafts for WikiAlpha and EverybodyWiki; and adapt well-sourced content for partner knowledge bases.

We also repurpose verified sources into press-ready summaries, structured data packages, and content that strengthens future Wikipedia submissions. Tell us your goals and we'll recommend and execute a tailored cross-platform strategy to maximize visibility and long-term credibility.
